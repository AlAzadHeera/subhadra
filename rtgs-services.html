RTGS SERVICES

Bank is offering facility to remit funds transfer Realtime between Banks across India. RTGS is the fastest possible mode of money transfer through bank channels available in India. RTGS facility is primarily for large value remittances. The minimum amount to be remitted through RTGS is Rs. 2 Lakhs.


Customers may remit any amount using RTGS facility available at the branch.
Full account number of the Beneficiary
Name of the Beneficiary
IFSC (Indian Financial System Code)of the Beneficiary Bank/Branch

Frequently Asked Questions

What is RTGS?
The National Electronic Funds Transfer System (NEFT) is a system which provide facility to transfer funds electronically from your account with us to another account in other Banks anywhere in India
Is there any minimum/maximum limit with regard to amount to be remit?
Minimum amount required to transfer is Rs. 2 Lakhs. There is no upper limit. Any amount can be transferred
Can the money be transferred to any Bank in the country?
No. Money can be transferred to only those Branches of Banks where this RTGS facility is enabled. You need to check with Beneficiary whether their Bank is having RTGS facility before initiating remittance from our Bank.
What care should be taken while originating a RTGS transaction?
The following should be ensured while putting through a funds transfer transaction using RTGS –

Originating and destination bank branches are part of the RTGS network.

Beneficiary details such as beneficiary name, account number and account type, name and IFSC of the beneficiary bank branch should be available with the remitter.

Extreme care should be exercised in providing the account number of the beneficiary, as, in the course of processing RTGS transactions, the credit will be given to the customer’s account solely based on the account number provided in the RTGS remittance instruction / message.
How do I know whether the Branch where the money is to be remit is enabled for RTGS?
You can either get the information from website https://www.rbi.org.in/scripts/neft.aspx or you may get the information from the Bank where you are operating the account.
Can I receive / send remittances from NRI Accounts?
Yes. Subject to applicability of provisions of FEMA
What is the procedures to transfer the funds through NEFT?
Approach the nearest branch and fill up the application Form wherein you have to give the details of your account no, amount to be transferred, beneficiaries name, Bank and Branch and IFSC code of the beneficiary bank
What is UTR number?
Unique Transaction Reference (UTR) number is a 22 character code used to uniquely identify a transaction in RTGS system

Frequently Asked Questions

What is IFSC Code and where would I get it ?
Indian Financial System Code (IFSC) is a unique code of 11 digits of the NEFT enabled Branches. Normally, this code will be printed on the cheque books or it may be obtained from the nearest branch of our Bank.
How long will it take to reach funds to beneficiary ?
Beneficiary would get the funds on the same day or the next day depending on the time of remittance.
Can I send remittances abroad through NEFT?
No. It is applicable to within the country
Can I receive Remittances from abroad to my account through NEFT?
No.
Can I initiate Stop fund transfer for NEFT?
Once the transaction is put through, you cannot stop the fund transfer. You have to approach Beneficiary Bank/Branch.
Can I Received the funds from my clients in the same way?
Once the transaction is put through, you cannot stop the fund transfer. You have to approach Beneficiary Bank/Branch.
How do I come to know about the funds transfer?
Either you can get the confirmation from the beneficiary himself OR from your branch, from where the transaction is initiated.
In case of non-credit or delay in credit to beneficiary’s account, whom should I contact?
You can contact the Branch itself from where you have requested for funds transfer.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which are the securities against which Secured Loan/Overdraft can be availed from Bank?
You may take Loan/Overdraft against Fixed deposit, NSC, LIC and KVP.
What are the tenure options for Loan/Overdraft against Fixed deposit/KVP/NSC/LIC?
You can repay your loan over a period of 12 to 60 months depending upon the maturity of the securities. Overdraft is a running account.
How can I repay my Loan/Overdraft against Fixed deposit/KVP/NSC/LIC?
The loan can be paid through ECS channel. Further, you can also pay through a standing instruction by debit to your Subhadra Bank account as per your convenience, however on the maturity date of securities the amount t outstanding would be recovered in full from the maturity proceeds. In case of overdraft as it is a running account there would be continuous debits/credits and the overdraft facility can be fully repaid from the maturity proceeds of the securities. In case the overdraft facility is further required substitute, securities can be given to the Bank.
Can I foreclose or prepay my Loan?
You can foreclose/prepay the entire loan outstanding anytime.
Do I need to open an account with Subhadra Bank for availing and / or servicing the loan/overdraft?
We will encourage you to start a Banking relationship with us to get the opportunity of other value added services being offered time to time on respective products. Opening an account with us is mandatory.
